    Live call script

    1. Ask: “What types of services do you have?” The answer should be short and ask what you are dealing with.
    2. Say: “I have ants in the kitchen.” Paul should show light empathy and ask a practical ant question.
    3. Complete phone booking with name, caller number confirmation, address, preferred time, availability, echo verification, and Cal.com booking.
    4. Ask for pricing. Paul should not quote a fake price.
    5. Ask for a live person once. Paul should offer scheduling first unless urgent; if you insist, he should transfer.
    6. Urgent hornet or sting issues should transfer quickly.
