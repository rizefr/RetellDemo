The real problem

Late invoices are a follow-up problem, not an accounting problem.

Most overdue accounts do not need pressure. They need a clear, consistent reminder with the exact amount and an easy way to pay — and that reminder keeps getting bumped by busier work.

01

Unpaid invoices sit for weeks.

02

03

When nobody answers, the next business that picks up wins the conversation. After-hours callers rarely leave a voicemail and wait.

FAQ

Practical questions before you automate customer follow-up.

The audit is built to answer whether a workflow should be automated, what should stay human, and what needs to be tested before customers experience it.

How much does it cost?

Pricing is flexible and based on workflow scope, call volume, integrations, and rollout complexity. The AI Audit sizes the work before anything is built, so you see the scope before committing to anything.

Will this replace my team?

No. Elixis systems take on repetitive follow-up and intake so staff can focus on the work customers actually pay for. Disputes, exceptions, and judgment calls are routed to people.

Will customers dislike talking to AI?

Some customers prefer a person, which is why each workflow stays narrow, uses scripts your business approves, is tested before launch, and routes to a human quickly when the conversation needs one.

Is this for consumer debt collection?

No. Elixis Collect is positioned for first-party B2B invoice follow-up. It is not for consumer debt collection, medical debt, financial debt, or legal collection work.

Does the AI collect card details?

No. The system should not collect verbal card details. The safer pattern is a professional reminder plus the secure payment path your business already approves.

Will it work with my existing systems?

Usually the first audit step is to review how invoice and customer data can be exported, connected, or staged. The exact integration depends on your current tools and permissions.

What happens when someone disputes an invoice?

The workflow should flag disputes, confusion, and exceptions for human review. It should not negotiate, offer payment plans, or make policy exceptions by default.

Can I pause or control it?

Yes. That is the intended operating model: outcomes stay visible, and outreach rules include pause, stop, and manual-review paths before launch.
