First step · Elixis AI Audit

Book an AI Audit

We identify the first workflow worth improving, define the guardrails, and decide whether Elixis Collect, Intake, or a custom Elixis System is the right starting point.

01

Find the workflow with the clearest payoff

Bring unpaid invoices, missed-call issues, lead capture gaps, service routing problems, or a repetitive admin process. We will focus on the part that wastes the most time first.

02

Define what stays human

The goal is not to replace your team or rebuild everything at once. We set scripts, pause rules, escalation paths, and review points before any workflow goes live.

03

Pressure-test fit, cost, and complexity

We look at data sources, integrations, call volume, customer expectations, and operational risk so the first system is practical instead of overbuilt.

What we will clarify

A useful audit answers the questions owners usually have before automation.

How much does it cost?Pricing is flexible and based on workflow scope, call volume, integrations, and rollout complexity. The audit sizes the work before anything is built.
Will this replace my team?No. The first system should support your team by handling repetitive follow-up, and surface exceptions clearly for people to decide.
Will customers dislike AI?Each workflow stays narrow, uses scripts you approve, is tested before launch, and routes to a human quickly when the conversation needs one.
Will it work with our existing systems?The audit reviews how your invoice and customer data can be exported, connected, or staged with your current tools and permissions.
Can we pause or control it?Yes. Outcomes stay visible, and outreach rules include pause, stop, and manual-review paths before launch.
What if our workflow is not a fit?Then the audit says so. Some workflows should stay human, and it is better to learn that in a review than after a build.
